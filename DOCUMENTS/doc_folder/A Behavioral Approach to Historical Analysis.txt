To issue a clarion call for historians to adopt a behavioral approach to their subject may seem as up-to-date and exciting as last month's newspaper, for it is the academic fashion today to argue the utility of social science theory in the writing of history.  Books and articles issue forth from the presses in seeming profusion advocating and even occasionally demonstrating such an approach.  Yet these many pages fail in three significant ways to accomplish the end they nominally espouse and which I urge.
