The question of librarianship as a profession is considered here in terms of the three key relationships of a professional-client, organizational and professional.. Professional practice in this field is thus cast against accepted norms and standards of professional behavior.. This critical assessment suggests that librarianship falls far short of the professional model.. Major shifts in the nature of the services performed by librarians and in their bureaucratic relationships will be required if librarianship is to advance.. The contributions of the professional associations and of library schools to the advancement of the process of professionalization is also analyzed.. Progress in the field is viewed to be inextricably tied to the success or failure which librarianship achieves in its quest for true professional attainment..
